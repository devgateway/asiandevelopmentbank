    Ecotourism and Agriculture Help Save Wetlands Habitat in the People's Republic of China

    In the Sanjiang Plain, People’s Republic of China, ADB has helped to restore thousands of hectares of wetlands while protecting and improving the livelihoods of farmers.
  • Project Result / Case Study4 May 2015

    Clean Water Improves Health, Breaks Down Barriers in Nepal

    A community-based project supported by ADB has brought clean water and better sanitation to more than 568,000 people in Nepal.
  • Project Result / Case Study3 May 2015

    Tourism Boom Fuels Business Opportunities in Mekong Subregion

    ADB has helped turn former war-torn Greater Mekong Subregion countries of Cambodia, Lao PDR, and Viet Nam into a booming tourist destination.
  • Project Result / Case Study2 May 2015

    Better Banking Practices Boost Business, Create Jobs in Azerbaijan’s Diversifying Economy

    ADB’s partnership with an Azerbaijan bank has promoted better banking practices and helped entrepreneurs flourish in a country that has rocketed to upper middle-income status.
  • Project Result / Case Study16 Mar 2015

    Building Roads and a Community Spirit in Indonesia's Lampung Province

    On the Indonesian island of Sumatra, constructing a rural road became the opportunity for a rural community to develop the local economy and take charge of its own future.
  • Project Result / Case Study26 Feb 2015

    Banking Services Put Women in Business in Papua New Guinea

    In Papua New Guinea, a microfinance project delivering banking services to the poor supports the financial and social empowerment of women.
  • Project Result / Case Study20 Feb 2015

    Investing in Irrigation Improves Agricultural Yields in North Viet Nam

    Three irrigation systems  developed in Viet Nam’s Red River Delta with assistance from ADB and Agence Française de Développement (AFD) have helped improve the livelihood of farming communities.

    Clean Energy Keeps Sri Lanka's Lights on

    Sri Lanka has been a success story in bringing reliable electricity supply to its people, reaching a 98 percent electrification rate.
